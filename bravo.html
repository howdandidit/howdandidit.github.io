Key Ingredients

What's Inside Bravo

Every ingredient sourced from certified Swiss and European suppliers. The yogurt kit contains three component blends combined during home fermentation.

Organic Bovine Colostrum
Sourced from Swiss cows under Demeter Biodynamic standards. Rich in immunoglobulins, lactoferrin, and growth factors — the unique fermentation medium that makes Bravo unlike any other probiotic.
Bifidobacterium Complex
Multiple strains including B. infantis, B. bifidum, B. lactis, and B. longum — critical for gut immunity, anti-inflammatory activity, and gut-associated lymphoid tissue function.
Lactobacillus Array
Includes L. bulgaricus, L. acidophilus, L. rhamnosus, L. salivarius, L. paracasei, and L. casei — supporting digestion, lactose metabolism, and competitive exclusion of harmful organisms.
Kefir Culture Grains
Traditional fermentation starter combining Lactobacillus, Streptococcus thermophilus, and yeast cultures — producing a complex array of organic acids and bioactive compounds.
Lactoferrin & Immunoglobulins
Naturally present in colostrum. Lactoferrin is a multifunctional glycoprotein associated with immune modulation and antimicrobial activity in the gut environment.
Bacteriophages
Micro-organisms that colonize inside gut bacteria — forming their own microbiome layer. Bravo is one of the only products to include phages as a deliberate component.

Dr. Ruggiero is a molecular biologist and immunologist who spent decades researching the human microbiome. His core insight: restoring microbiome diversity — not just adding a few bacterial strains — is the key to supporting healthy gut and immune function.

Bravo was developed after hundreds of formulation trials, with the goal of creating a product that could reproduce the complexity of a naturally healthy gut microbiome in a way no probiotic supplement had previously achieved.

The use of bovine colostrum as a fermentation medium — combined with the proprietary multi-strain culture array — produces unique postbiotic compounds that emerge from fermentation itself. This is why Bravo describes itself not as a probiotic, but as a Microbiome Restoration System.
